Background

Thermal Engineering Design for Additive Manufacturing

Qdot Additive LLC uses advanced simulation tools and years of know-how in additive manufacturing to design innovative structures for heat transfer applications, from HVAC&R to high temperature recuperators and power generation applications.

Qdot is a spin-off from a University of Wisconsin project funded by the Department of Energy which focused on thermally conductive polymer composite heat exchangers for air-to-water applications found in industrial HVAC&R. Building on this expertise, Qdot leverages its experience in thermal design and simulation, along with design for additive manufacturing, to create innovative, manufacturable heat transfer designs.

Projects

Polymer Composite Air-to-Water Heat Exchanger

Qdot Additive designed a low pressure drop air-to-water heat exchanger for HVAC applications. It is manufactured by fused filament fabrication using specially designed toolpathing and highly effective convective features. The material is a thermally conductive polycarbonate (PC) composite that was specially developed for the application. PC can withstand temperatures above 100 °C and is resistant to many caustic chemicals.

Stainless Steel Air-to-Water Heat Exchanger

Metal additive manufacturing is the answer for high effectiveness, compact heat exchangers in demanding environments with high pressures, high temperatures, or certain corrosive environments. Qdot Additive designed a low pressure drop air-to-water heat exchanger for HVAC applications as a proof of concept. It is manufactured by the direct metal laser sintering (DMLS) process using stainless steel 316L.

Thermal Test Data

These heat exchangers were designed for an operating air-side pressure drop of 40 Pa and inlet temperature difference of 69.4 °C for water and air in crossflow. The data are scaled for a 12 in. x 12 in. frontal area.
